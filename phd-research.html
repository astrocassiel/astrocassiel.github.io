PhD Research

Wire-Arc Additive Manufacturing, thermal monitoring, and control for advanced aerospace manufacturing.

Research Projects

PhD Research – Carnegie Mellon University

Developing real-time thermal monitoring and control for WAAM processes using two-color thermography with a single camera. Research integrates thermal imaging, process parameter tuning, and machine feedback to enable closed-loop defect mitigation.

  • WAAM & CMT Process Monitoring
  • Two-Color Thermography
  • Cooling Rate & Hardness Correlation
  • MATLAB, LabVIEW, Python + Julia Integration

NASA Marshall Space Flight Center (Summer 2025)

Applied two-color imaging to NASA's Cold Metal Transfer system for in-situ melt pool monitoring. Supported process control exploration on lunar regolith simulants and other DED systems. Developed tools for tech transfer between academia and NASA.

  • WAAM Melt Pool Imaging
  • Regolith DED Monitoring
  • Hardware & Sensor Integration
